Toronto

43.6532° N, 79.3832° W

I used to wish for better days.

These days, there are no bad days.

I stared straight, in front watching the red sky. Moving and motionless towards the setting sun. I felt relieved to be leaving and hopeful. Staring at the sun, I hoped it would envelope me whole, the rays filling me up with the brightness and warmth of all that is hopeful and new. The train kept going forward, further west, never truly being engulfed by that setting star. Nonetheless, in a few short hours, I would be with friends, talking about our dreams, tasting and drinking like it was the first time. I've always wanted to like Toronto. On paper it is everything I want in a place: busy, cosmopolitan, busy, stylish, and international. But it never gripped me like other places. This time, it rocked my world and enveloped me in that hopefulness and possibility that a big, busy and bustling city can.
