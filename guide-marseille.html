Marseille

43.2965° N, 5.3698° E

Knots in my stomach as the bus, with every sharp turn, goes higher and higher. I sit on steps at the top of Marseille. I let the sun kiss my skin, I let the wind caress my face. My fear of heights dissipates, I am in bliss. The next day starts early with a boat ride to the island of If. Above me, the Mediterranean sun is beaming out in the middle of the sea. I can see this drama being played out high above Château d'If. Suddenly, we are back on the water, picking up speed and the cold wind.

I study the passengers in front of me, young olive-skinned boys off work, joking and lighting cigarettes when one of them sitting behind me starts up a conversation. I am intrigued. It is evening now on the water. We find a calm English pub but I am too excited to sit down, I want to move my body. Over beers and talks about family and home, he holds my hand softly on his lap while his stubble tickles my cheek. Trembling, I count the hours before the return up north. Twenty-four.

To stay:

We stayed in the loveliest Airbnb in the neighbourhood of La Villette in the city centre. Next time, I would like to stay near the 9e and 10e arrondissement, closer to the Parc national des Calanques to take advantage of hiking trials and the beaches nearby.

To do:

Notre Dame-de-la-Grade and La Cité Radieuse (both free admissions), they offer the most stunning views of the city. I would suggest lingering there, bring some lunch and a book to read, sit on the ground, feel the sea breeze, let the sun kiss your cheeks.
